History of London



A Brief History of London London was founded by the Romans around AD 43 and was originally called Londinium. It quickly grew into an important trading port. After the fall of the Roman Empire, the city declined but was later revived by the Anglo-Saxons. In 1066, William the Conqueror invaded England, and London became the political center of the country.

The Tower of London was built shortly after to secure his control. During the medieval period, London grew in population and importance. The city suffered greatly during the Black Death in the 14th century, and later during the Great Fire of London in 1666, which destroyed much of the city.
